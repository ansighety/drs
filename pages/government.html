Structure

Despite the existence of a monarchy in the empire, the Government of The Inviolable Holy Empire functions similarly to a constitutional monarchy and parliamentary republic which consists of the below 3 branches:

  • Executive
  • Legislative
  • Judiciary
Executive

The Executive branch, also known as the Cabinet, executes and enforces the law, and has overall responsibility for the governance of the Empire. This branch consists of the following main members that are collectively responsible to the Legislative:

  • Head of State: The Emperor/Empress
  • Head of Government: The Emperor/Empress Consort
  • Cabinet: The High Council

The Cabinet is made up of the High Council which consists of 2 representatives from each of the 11 Courts aside from the Sacraaliag court, thus adding up to a total of 22 Concillors. The Council is further divided into 4 key areas:

The Treasury Council oversees the Empire's treasury and finances. The Courts that fall under this Council are the (Trade & Finance), Jordem and (Foreign Affairs) Courts.

The Affairs Council oversees the law and justice of the Empire, and is also in charge of the Empire's intelligence. The Courts that fall under this Council are the Nyskandereit, Raadserium and Verisjon Courts.

The Defenses Council oversees the protection of the Empire and the Sacred House. The Courts that fall under this Council are the Grondkule, Zeehav and Heimvander Courts.

Legislative

The Legislative branch, also known as the Parliament, holds the authority to make laws for the Empire and may observe or steer governing actions, with the authority to amend and control the Empire's finances. They are in charge of keeping the Executive's actions in check.

Legislators, which are the members of the Legislative branch, are typically elected from the Almechting Church every 10 years, with the Church's High Priest always leading the Legislative. It is also possible for the Executive to nominate legislators.

This branch consists of the following main members:

  • Head of Parliament: The High Priest
  • Parliament: Legislators
Judiciary

The Judiciary branch, also known as the Judicature, is a system of judiciary courts that adjudicates legal disputes/disagreements, interprets, defends and applies the laws passed by the Legislative in legal cases. This branch operates independently from the Legislative and Executive, and its judges are all apointed by the Head of State with advice from the Head of Government.

This branch consists of two tiers of judiciary courts:

  • Supreme Court
  • State Courts

The Supreme Court consists of the High Court and the Court of Appeals. The High Court hears crimes that are considered serious or severe, and civil cases with claims exceeding $250,000. The Court of Appeals hears all of the appeals against any decision made by the High Court.

The State Courts consist of the following smaller judiciary courts:

  • District Courts
  • Magistrate Courts
  • Juvenile Courts
  • Coroners’ Courts
  • Small Claims Tribunals
